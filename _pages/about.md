Ziyadat Ekundayo is a fourth-year Ph.D. student in the Department of 
Economics at Wayne State University. Her research lies at the intersection 
of Health Economics, Labor Economics, and Family Economics. She focuses on 
the impacts of health shocks, family spillover effects, and the role of 
paid leave policies in the United States. Her current projects examine the effects of paid sick leave on children's 
developmental and behavioral health outcomes, and the impacts of paid family leave 
on healthcare utilization among caregivers. Her research employs 
quasi-experimental methods to identify causal policy effects, with 
implications for the design of family and medical leave policy.

## Research Interests
Health Economics, Labor Economics, Development Economics
